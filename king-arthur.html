King Arthur
Story Theory & Controversy Lessons to Learn Influence on Society

A world of chivalry, magic, & the quest for the legendary sword, Excalibur

Posted by Nixon Ooi on Jan 03, 2022

This is a story of many, from the monumental drawing of the sword known as Caliburn to the knights of the round table or to finding the holy grail. King Arthur transcends through centuries into the hearts of those whom are told the tale.

It’s as if he was destined to become a warrior and eventually king, slaying giants and witches alongside other brave characters of his time. Unity in strength was his upmost lessons known throughout the world. Despite the tragic ending of his life, much can be learnt and emanated from the story of King Arthur.


Theory & Controversy

Dating back to the 5th or 6th century, the story of Robin Hood was only given a new lease of life in the late 19th century when focus on the values he stood for instead of the political pawn he was made to be that represented the rights to rule.

Interestingly, the values though important is not sought after. The underlying pull towards king Arthur’s story is the existence of a visionary leadership that is inspirational and transformational in equal measure. Brings hope that it is attainable again, especially in this unsettling world

Lessons to Learn

Unity and Equality

The concept of the Round Table, where all knights were considered equal and there was no head of the table, symbolizes unity and equality. This teaches us the importance of working together harmoniously, valuing every individual's contribution, and promoting a sense of equality and fraternity.

Loyalty and Friendship

The loyalty between Arthur and his knights, especially exemplified in the friendship with Sir Lancelot and Sir Gawain, teaches the value of unwavering loyalty and true friendship. Loyalty to one's friends and companions is a virtue celebrated in Arthurian legends.

Forgiveness and Redemption

The story of Sir Lancelot and Queen Guinevere illustrates themes of forgiveness and redemption. Despite their flaws and mistakes, they find forgiveness and strive for redemption, teaching the value of forgiveness, self-reflection, and the possibility of redemption even after wrongdoing.

Personal Sacrifice

Arthur's eventual fate, often depicted as his journey to Avalon or his rest until Britain needs him again, signifies personal sacrifice for the greater good. It highlights the idea that leaders sometimes must sacrifice their personal desires for the well-being of their people.



Influence on society

Religion

Christian ideals and Arthurian notions of medieval chivalry are often featured.

Camelot

It is the legendary castle and court associated with King Arthur that has become a symbol of a utopian, idyllic society.

Pop-Culture

Arthurian legends have been adapted into numerous movies, TV shows, video games, and other forms of popular culture.
