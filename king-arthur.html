A world of chivalry, magic, & the quest for the legendary sword, Excalibur

In many versions of the legend, the sword that Arthur pulls from the stone as a boy is named Caliburn. The best of King Arthur's Knights of the Round Table is often said to be Sir Launcelot, but it is Sir Galahad who finds the Holy Grail, proving himself worthy of the title of the greatest knight.

He was a warrior, a knight and a king who killed giants, witches and monsters and led a band of heroes on many daring adventures. He is known for his Knights of the Round Table and for uniting the peoples of his land. Even though his end was tragic, he is still known and celebrated all over the world today.


Theory & Controversy

The Arthurian revival of the late 19th century, for example, helped put him back on the international cultural map by removing the historical aura, and emphasising the values he stood for – a far cry from the medieval attempts to utilise him as a national figure from whom medieval kings could derive their right to rule. This paved the way to the fantasy worlds created, most famously, by T.H. White in The Once and Future King, published in 1958.

The dreams of yesterday are the hopes of today and the reality of tomorrow. Science has not yet mastered prophecy. We predict too much for the next year and yet far too little for the next ten.

All of these interpretations were about more than just revealing the secrets of one of the most intriguing men of all time. In this confusing and sometimes frightening world, audiences seek reassurance in the models of the past. They want a standard of moral integrity and visionary leadership that is inspirational and transformational in equal measure. One that they cannot find in the world around them, but will discover in the stories of King Arthur.

They offer the reassurance that doing the morally right thing is valuable, even if it may bring about temporary defeat. In the end, virtues and values prevail and it is these enduring features of the legends that have kept them alive in the hearts and minds of so many through the centuries.

Lessons to Learn (snippet stories)

Unity and Equality

The concept of the Round Table, where all knights were considered equal and there was no head of the table, symbolizes unity and equality. This teaches us the importance of working together harmoniously, valuing every individual's contribution, and promoting a sense of equality and fraternity.

Loyalty and Friendship

The loyalty between Arthur and his knights, especially exemplified in the friendship with Sir Lancelot and Sir Gawain, teaches the value of unwavering loyalty and true friendship. Loyalty to one's friends and companions is a virtue celebrated in Arthurian legends.

The Consequences of Greed

In some versions of the myth, kelpies were said to grant wishes or offer rides to those who tried to mount them. This emphasizes the consequences of greed; desiring too much or taking shortcuts can lead to disastrous outcomes.

Courage and Honor

The knights of the Round Table, including Arthur himself, are known for their bravery and chivalry. They face challenges and adversaries with courage and honor, showcasing the importance of these qualities in the face of adversity.

Forgiveness and Redemption

The story of Sir Lancelot and Queen Guinevere illustrates themes of forgiveness and redemption. Despite their flaws and mistakes, they find forgiveness and strive for redemption, teaching the value of forgiveness, self-reflection, and the possibility of redemption even after wrongdoing.

Personal Sacrifice

Arthur's eventual fate, often depicted as his journey to Avalon or his rest until Britain needs him again, signifies personal sacrifice for the greater good. It highlights the idea that leaders sometimes must sacrifice their personal desires for the well-being of their people.
