In 1852, Dr John Snow (1813–1858) found cholera deaths linked to contaminated water from public pumps, including the one by his favourite pub on Broad Street.

In the 1800s, cholera spread through dirty water, sickening people and causing serious stomach problems. Like the COVID-19 precautions taken from 2020 to 2022, people wore masks and were careful. Cholera is still a problem today in crowded places and after disasters when there is no clean water. It is treatable, but it still kills many people every year.
