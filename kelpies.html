Kelpies
Story Theory & Controversy Lessons to Learn Influence on Society

Where water holds ancient secrets & mythical wonders await

Posted by Nixon Ooi on July 15, 2023

A kelpie, aka water kelpie, is a shape shifting spirit that inhabits water bodies stooped in Scottish folklore. Described as a black horse creature that shape-shift's a human form.

A distinct trait of a kelpie are it’s reversed hooves. Sitting by the side of water bodies, the enchantment of their singing lures humans towards them.


Theory & Controversy

Legend has it once caught in their spell, it is near impossible to break free from the immense strength they possess similar to a horse. This sheds light on kelpies as a symbol of power used during Scotlands Industrial Revolution.

The origin stories of these shape-shifters are believed to come from those who pray and partake in human sacrifices to the water gods. However, it is now used to dissuade children’s from wandering into the water bodies unsupervised. And also women to steer clear from good looking strangers.

Kelpies are usually considered evil however a story from the island of Barra in the Outer Hebrides details the efforts of a lonely kelpie that was looking for love and so shape shifted into a handsome man to help win the heart of a women who he wanted to take as his wife.

Date of Origin

Pictish stones from the 6th to 9th centuries features representations of a kelpie.

Location: Scottland

“He said: Are you my sacrifice, sweet flesh? How pale and young you are. Tell me, are they resuming the sacrifices to the water once more?”

Lessons to Learn

Beware of Deception

The deaths of unsuspecting humans brings about the dangers of deception and the importance of being cautious, especially when something appears too good to be true.

Trust Your Instincts

Kelpie's victims were able to sense something was wrong but ignored their instincts. Trusting your intuition and being aware of your surroundings can prevent you from getting in trouble.

The Consequences of Greed

Kelpies were said to grant wishes or offer rides to those who tried to mount them. This emphasizes the consequences of greed; desiring too much or taking shortcuts can lead to disastrous outcomes.

Respect Nature

Kelpies were often portrayed as nature spirits associated with bodies of water. This myth teaches us to respect nature and its elements. Disrespecting nature can have dire consequences.



Influence on society

Kelpies in Writing

Sir Walter Scott’s The Bride of Lammermoor (1818) contain two logical explanations for the origin of belief in kelpies; water-spouts moving across lochs and quicksands. Kelpies are also mentioned in books by J.K. Rowling.

Kelpies in painting

Kelpies were a popular theme for artists around the beginning of the 20th century including Herbert Draper whose version shows a relatively harmless nymph. It is recorded that the painting received a mixed reception.

Kelpies in sculptures

Kelpies can be seen as distant descendants of the Pictish beasts found on early medieval sculptured stones particularly in the Scottish north-east. Pictish beasts come in a variety of shapes.
