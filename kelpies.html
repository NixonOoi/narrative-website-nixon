Lessons to Learn (snippet stories)

Beware of Deception

Kelpies were believed to appear as beautiful horses to lure unsuspecting humans, especially children, into the water, where they would then drag them under and drown them. This warns us about the dangers of deception and the importance of being cautious, especially when something appears too good to be true.

Trust Your Instincts

In many stories, the kelpie's victims were able to sense something was wrong but ignored their instincts. Trusting your intuition and being aware of your surroundings can prevent you from falling into dangerous situations.

The Consequences of Greed

In some versions of the myth, kelpies were said to grant wishes or offer rides to those who tried to mount them. This emphasizes the consequences of greed; desiring too much or taking shortcuts can lead to disastrous outcomes.

Respect Nature

Kelpies were often portrayed as nature spirits associated with bodies of water. This myth teaches us to respect nature and its elements. Ignoring or disrespecting nature can have dire consequences, just as ignoring the warnings about the kelpie led to tragedy.

The power of stories

Myths and folklore are powerful tools for conveying important life lessons. The story of the kelpie serves as a cautionary tale, reminding us of the dangers that exist in the world and the importance of passing down wisdom through stories.

Parental Guidance

The kelpie myth is often used to caution children about the dangers of wandering near water bodies alone. It underscores the importance of parental guidance and the need for children to heed their parents' warnings and advice.

Symbol of Transformation

In some interpretations, the kelpie represents transformation and change. This can remind us that change is a natural part of life and can be both beautiful and frightening. Embracing change and being prepared for its challenges is a valuable life lesson.
