Robin Hood
Story Theory & Controversy Lessons to Learn Influence on Society

Legendary outlaw, defender of the poor

Posted by Nixon Ooi on August 24, 2019

As stories are retold, different versions of Robin’s upbringing surfaces. Some depicted him coming from Nobel birth others shows him as a member of the yeoman (servant) class. What we are sure of is that he was a highly skilled archer and swordsman hailed from England’s Sherwood Forest of Nottingham. Adorned in cloths of Lincoln green, he steals from the rich to give to the poor.

Robins cause did not come without pushback particularly from the towns sheriff. Fortunately, he has won the hearts of the people whom they call leader. Constantly supplying him with information to evade the clutches of the law.

The sheriff devised a plan to capture Robin Hood by luring him with his favourite activity, archery. A competition where the best archer would win a golden arrow as a prize. Robin took the bait, ignoring warnings from his closest confidants. But alas the solidarity of the people showed when they wore Lincoln green, hiding Robin hood in plain sight. The sheriff shot the closest arrow just shy of a bullseye when comes Robin’s arrow dead centre. The frenzy from the crowd allowed Robin Hood to make his escape before the sheriff realised his true identity.


Theory & Controversy

There are 2 school of thoughts when it comes to the story of Robin Hood. Those that see him as an embodiment of their spirit, generosity and courage. And those in contrast sees more disruption than aid in pushing for a fairer society.

Date of Origin

Robin Hood real name was Earl of Huntington (Est 1160 - 1198)

Location: Sherwood Forest

"We never rob. We just sort of borrow a bit from those who can afford it."

Lessons to Learn

Have a clear plan

Prince John cruelly increases taxes, which the poor villagers cannot pay. Robin comes up with a plan: He and his Merry Men will rob the oppressing nobles and their overlord Prince John and give their wealth to the poor.

Harness your team’s excellence

Robin encounters Little John (and is defeated by him) and Friar Tuck (and fights to a draw with him). Robin, instead of feeling intimidated by these men, recognizes their talent. He asks them both to join him in his fight against the Sheriff of Nottingham.

Get the inspiration going

Now that Robin has a team together, he sets about robbing the rich to give to the poor of Nottingham. But his merry men are outnumbered and outarmed. So Robin Hood gathers them together and inspires them with his words and purpose.

Enjoy what you do

Their is to steal from the rich and give to the poor, that means they have absolutely no profit. But they pursue the mission with astounding zeal, gleefully giving away all of their stolen riches (their profits).



Influence on society

The Robin Hood Effect

Occured when the less well-off gain economically at the expense of the better-off. For example, our government bodies uses tax revenue to provide services for the poor.

Symbol of Hope

Robin Hood is a symbol of the spirit of the common man standing up against tyranny and injustice. These are timeless and universal concepts and are why he has resonance throughout the world.

Box Office Hit

In fact, the first Robin Hood film, Robin Hood and His Merry Men, was a silent movie released in 1908. It's one of six silent films based on the character that were released--and over 70 adaptations to movies and TV as a whole.
